# Centry Ecosystem — Website Design Spec

## Overview

**Brand:** Centry
**Tagline options (pick one):**
- "Your business, finally in focus."
- "Built by operators. Powered by AI."
- "The operating system for Vietnamese SMEs."
- "Stop managing chaos. Start running your business."

**What it is:** An AI-powered ecosystem of three tools (CARA, Centricom, Centrify) that replaces the Zalo-group-chat-and-Excel-screenshot workflow that most Vietnamese SMEs run on today.

**Primary audience:** SME owners/founders (Vietnam first, international second) — people running 2-50 person businesses across services, retail, education, F&B, wellness, clinics, etc.
**Secondary audience:** Operations managers, franchise operators, tech-curious business consultants

**Core positioning:** Centry is NOT another SaaS tool built by engineers who've never run a business. It was built by a founder who operates 8+ physical locations and couldn't find anything that worked for how Vietnamese businesses actually operate. The real competitor is Zalo + Excel + gut feeling — not Odoo or Base.vn.

**Language:** English (primary). Vietnamese can be added as a toggle in v2.

**Vibe:** Modern, confident, slightly bold. NOT corporate/enterprise (that's the PPG site). NOT playful/cute (that's Pamper Me). Think: smart operator energy. Clean, fast, credible. A founder should land on this site and think "these people get it."

## Brand Identity

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Deep charcoal/near-black (#111111) — modern, authoritative
- **Secondary:** White (#FFFFFF) — clean, spacious
- **Accent:** Electric teal/cyan (#00D4AA) — signals AI, intelligence, clarity
- **Secondary accent:** Warm amber (#F59E0B) — for CTAs and highlights, energy
- **Muted backgrounds:** Off-white (#F8F8F6) or very light gray (#F3F4F6) for alternating sections
- **Subtle text:** Medium gray (#6B7280)

### Typography
- **Headlines:** A modern geometric sans like "Satoshi", "General Sans", "Cabinet Grotesk", or "Syne" — tech-forward but not cold
- **Body:** Clean readable sans like "DM Sans", "Plus Jakarta Sans", or "Outfit"
- **Code/data elements:** Monospace like "JetBrains Mono" or "IBM Plex Mono" for stats, metrics, anything that feels like data
- **Do NOT use:** Inter, Roboto, Arial, system fonts, or anything that looks like a generic SaaS template

### Visual Language
- Subtle grid patterns or dot grids in backgrounds — signals systems/structure
- Thin line illustrations or wireframe-style product mockups (not screenshots — the product may still be evolving)
- Data visualization motifs — small chart shapes, dashboard fragments as decorative elements
- NO stock photos of people pointing at screens
- NO generic "AI brain" or "robot" imagery
- Glassmorphism or subtle blur effects are OK if tasteful

## Design Principles

1. **Sell the outcome, not the feature.** Every section should answer "so what?" for a busy SME owner. They don't care about "event-sourced architecture" — they care about seeing their money clearly.

2. **Respect the reader's intelligence.** The audience runs businesses. Don't talk down to them. Don't use buzzwords. Write like you're talking to a sharp founder over coffee.

3. **Show, don't screenshot.** Use abstract product illustrations, data motifs, or wireframe-style mockups rather than actual product screenshots (unless the UI is truly polished and final). Abstract visuals age better and don't lock you into current UI.

4. **Speed is credibility.** The site should load fast. No heavy animations, no autoplay video, no cookie banner that blocks the page. A founder will bounce in 3 seconds if it feels slow or bloated.

5. **Mobile-first.** Many Vietnamese SME owners will see this site on their phone, sent via Zalo or Facebook. Every section must work perfectly on mobile.

6. **One CTA, repeated.** "Get Early Access" or "Book a Call" — pick one primary CTA and use it consistently. Don't confuse with multiple different asks.

## What NOT to Build (v1)

- No blog — use LinkedIn for content
- No documentation/knowledge base — too early
- No live chat widget
- No comparison page (vs. Odoo, Base.vn) — you're not competing with them, you're competing with Zalo + Excel
- No separate pages per product — keep it single-page for now. Separate product pages in v2 when each module has enough depth
- No Vietnamese language toggle in v1 — English signals international ambition. Add Vietnamese in v2
- No animations that delay content visibility

---

## Tone of Voice Guide (for all copy on the site)

**Do:**
- Write like a founder talking to another founder
- Use concrete examples ("your 47-person Zalo group" not "inefficient communication channels")
- Be direct and slightly irreverent
- Acknowledge the reality of how Vietnamese businesses actually work
- Use numbers when you have them

**Don't:**
- Use enterprise jargon ("leverage synergies," "digital transformation," "unlock value")
- Overpromise ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive")
- Sound like every other SaaS landing page
- Use "we" in a way that sounds like a 500-person company — you're a founder-led team, own it
- Apologize for being new — frame it as "we're building in the open" not "we're just starting out"
